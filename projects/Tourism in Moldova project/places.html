Orhei

Orhei is a town with municipal status in the central part of the Republic of Moldova, located 48 km north of Chisinau, on the bank of the river Răut. It is the administrative center of the district with the same name. Orhei is one of the main centers economy of the central region of the country.
Its name comes from the city of Orhei located on the Răut river, which is not very big, but despite all this it is beautiful and has everything necessary for human life in abundance. Lake Orhei is situated close to it towards the east, and the wonderful astron in this lake. On the west side of the lake in the middle of the woods, you can see the traces of an old fortress, called by the inhabitants Orheiul Vechi. Judging by its settlement, it seems to be the fortress of Petrodava in Dacia.


Chisinau

Chisinau is the capital, the largest city and the administrative, territorial, economic, scientific and cultural center of the Republic of Moldova.
Chisinau is connected by railways and roads with all municipalities, cities and district centers and many villages in the republic, also with urban centers in Romania, Ukraine, Bulgaria, Turkey, Belarus, Russia and other countries.
From an administrative point of view, it is divided into five sectors: Center, Botanica, Buiucani, Râșcani and Ciocana. The local body of state power is the City Hall of the municipality (Municipal Council). Chisinau is nicknamed the "White Stone City". The nickname comes from the abundance of light-colored buildings, being built of white stone limestone.


Soroca

Soroca is a city with municipal status in the northeast of the Republic of Moldova, located on the right bank of the Nistru River, on the border with Ukraine. It is the administrative center of the district of the same name.
With over 37 thousand inhabitants (2016), Soroca is the eighth largest city in Moldova, but also the second largest and most important in the Northern Region (being surpassed only by Balti). It is the administrative center of the district with the same name. In Soroca, there is a museum of history and ethnography that includes Cetatea Soroca, built in stone by Ștefan cel Mare in 1489 and renovated in its current form by Petru Rareș.


Old Orhei

The Old Orhei museum complex is located in the Răut valley, a right tributary of the Dniester, on the territory of the Trebujeni commune in the Orhei district of the Republic of Moldova.
The Orheiul Vechi cultural-natural reserve, which has a special status and is the most important cultural site in the Republic of Moldova, is also in the nomination process to be included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. The complex includes several tens of hectares of the medieval city of Orhei (XIII - XVI centuries), later named Orheiul Vechi (after the settlement was abandoned and a new town was founded elsewhere, with the same name - today's Orheiul). The complex includes two large headlands (Peștere and Butuceni), which are joined by three smaller adjacent headlands (Potarca, Selitra and Scoc), on whose territory are the ruins of fortifications, houses, baths, places of worship (including monasteries caves) both from the Tatar-Mongol period (XIII - XIV centuries) and Moldavian (XV - XVI centuries).
The Old Orheiul Complex constitutes a system made up of cultural and natural elements: archaic natural landscape, biodiversity, exceptional archaeological setting, historical-architectural variety, traditional rural habitat and ethnographic originality.


Cricova

Cricova is a town in Rîșcani sector, Chisinau municipality, Republic of Moldova, located 11 km from Chisinau. Thanks to over 120 km of natural galleries, mostly used for wine storage, Cricova is one of the biggest tourist attractions in the Republic of Moldova. Cricova is also nicknamed the city of sunshine.
Cricova has undergone many changes over time: socio-economic development, economic crises, wars and changes of rule, growth population and other important events. The last century in 2008 showed that the population had increased to 15,000 inhabitants.
Cricova is located on the Moldovenesc plateau with an altitude of 100-200 m, in the valley of the river Ichel. Orchards, fields, forests and fields meet here. The predominant soil is poorly humidified chernoze. The climate is temperate-continental, summers are hot and dry and cold and snowy winters.


Codrii

Codrul (plural of Codrii Moldovei) is a forest massif located in the hilly center of the Republic of Moldova, on the Central Moldavian Plateau, occupying approx. 40% from the surface of this high plateau. Forests mainly occupy the highest altitudes, between 200 and 430 m (Bălăneşti hill).
Codrul is mostly spread over the territory of the districts: Străseni, Hîncești, Călăraşi, Orhei and Nisporeni and, to a lesser extent, in: the west of Ungheni district, the north of Cimișlia district, the south of Telenești district and the north/southwest of Ialoveni district.
Among others, the oldest monasteries in the Republic of Moldova are located on the territory of Codril: Vărzărești (14th century), Căpriana (14th-15th century), Hâncu (17th century), Hârbovăț (18th century), Hârjăuca (18th century), Curchi (18th century), Condrița, etc.
